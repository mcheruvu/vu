Program Catalog

We offer two programs - (A) Doctorate / PhD in Applied Vedic Sciences and (B) Certification Course in Vēdas and Shāstrās.

The details of courses and credits for each program are provided in the student handbook.
One will get the student handbook when you request for the application.

  • A. Doctorate / PhD in Applied Vedic Sciences

    You can take your education to the next level with a doctorate in Applied Vēdic Sciences. After earning this degree, you may choose to put your education into practice in real Vēdic life.

    Coursework often helps students grow not only academically, but also spiritually. Topics covered include research in Upanishads, Darshanas, Ayurveda, Dhanurveda, Gandharvaveda, Shilpavidya or Arthaveda, Sri Ramayana, Mahabharata, Puranna, Brahmana, Aranyaka, Bhagavad Gita, Manu Shastra, spiritual disciplines, Yoga, Mathematics, Cosmology, social justice, and teaching methods etc.

    We are trying to acquire corporate funding for the research work in the areas of corporate’s interest. This may provide stipend for the Doctoral Student for a given period depending on the funding acquired.

    All promises of student support are subject to students making satisfactory academic progress, as determined by their programs of study.

    These are the general requirements for the Doctor of Philosophy:

    1. ADMISSION: The candidate must be formally admitted to his or her degree program.
    2. Minimum Qualification: The normal minimum qualification for the Ph.D. program in Applied Vedic Sciences program is high school completion. Depending on the qualification the candidate acquired prior to joining the Ph.D. in Applied Vedic Sciences, some courses may be exempted. Look for complete details here.
    3. ADVANCEMENT TO CANDIDACY: A student is advanced to candidacy for the Ph.D. when he or she has completed satisfactorily all the requirements, departmental and general, requisite to beginning work on the dissertation. Candidacy is determined by the department or program of study and certified by the Registrar. All departments require a preliminary examination before advancing any student to candidacy. All departments also require a final examination or defense. The examination is conducted by professors in the department and by such other members of the faculty as may be appointed.
    4. DISSERTATION: The candidate must present a dissertation on a topic related to his or her area of specialization that presents the results of original research and gives evidence of excellent scholarship. His/her research work must be published in a peer reviewed journal as suggested by the Department. The dissertation must be approved by the professor or committee under whose direction it is written and by the Graduate Council. All requirements for the Ph.D. must be completed within five years after advancement to candidacy.

    If a faculty member working with a doctoral student leaves the University of Applied Vedic Sciences (UAVS) for any reason before that student has completed his or her degree requirements, it may not always be possible for that faculty member to continue working with the student as an advisor. In such cases, departments will work with students to help them locate a new advisor.

    Individual departments and programs may have additional requirements regarding the number of courses to be taken, proficiency in foreign languages, special examinations, and theses. The department should be consulted for specific information.
